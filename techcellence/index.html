TECHcellence

Overview

The TECHcellence initiative addresses challenges in computer science education accessibility. At SJSU, 4,575 students applied to the Computer Science program in 2021, yet only 340 enrolled. The program creates a guided pathway from high school through San José City College to SJSU for underrepresented students.

Program Components

Summer Academy

A cohort of 90 low-income, first-generation high school students participates in a strengths-based summer program featuring:

  • Introductory computer science coursework
  • Faculty seminars with technology leaders
  • Mentoring activities led by SJSU undergraduates

Curricula Alignment

Faculty from Milpitas Unified School District, San José City College, and SJSU coordinate course offerings. Students can take dual enrollment CS courses through SJCC while in high school, funded by California's K12 Strong Workforce Grant.

Culturally Sustaining Mentorship

SJSU undergraduate computer science majors and aspiring K12 CS educators provide cohort-based mentoring, trained in culturally sustaining practices by Lurie College of Education faculty.

Partnership

TECHcellence is a collaboration among:

  • Milpitas Unified School District
  • San José City College
  • San José State University

The initial implementation received an APLU grant.
