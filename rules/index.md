# the 10 golden rules for success as a CMPE student

1. **ask questions!**
   at SJSU you have professors who are experts in their domain.
   they also have a desire to share their knowledge with you.
   initially, that will be through lectures, but you can get lectures on youtube that are much more entertaining.
   unlike youtube, you have a the expert right in front of you that you can ask when you don't understand.
   so, if you don't understand, **ask the question**.
   you may be scared that the question is stupid, but there will probably be many students in the class who will be happy that you asked.
2. **be in class!**
   don't skip class!
   we don't take attendance at SJSU, so it's easy to skip class, but you will miss out on a big part of learning.
   a good class will provide you with reading material, assignments, and lecture.
   lectures can emphasize and review key concepts in a different format from the readings --- they give you a chance to let you see the subject a different way.
   prepare for lectures by doing the reading before class.

3. **read the textbook!** 
4. **start the assignments on the day you get them!**
   assignments get harder the closer you get to the deadline.
   usually content needed for the assignment will be covered in class.
   if you are working on the assignment, while learning the subject you will:
   - apply the concepts as you learn them --- reinforcing the learning.
   - find gaps in your understanding when you get stuck --- you can ask questions in class to fill those gaps.
   - have time to make mistakes; figure out what the problem is; get it working --- one of the best learning cycles there is.
   
   a great workflow that a professor gave me in my abstract algebra class:
   
   1. start the assignment immediately and work on it until you are stuck
   2. do something else --- play video games, work on another class, go for a run, go to sleep
   3. work on the assignment again. you might finish. your subconscious will work on the problem and you will wake up with some insights --- remember this was a math prof, not a psycologist, so this might not be the best explanation for why it works :)
   4. if you don't make progress go to office hours!
5. **be on campus!**
   if you are arriving on campus just before your class and jetting out right after, you are missing out on much of the value of your university experience.
   you'll also often arrive late and frazzled to class.
   get to class an hour before your first class and plan on staying at least one our later.
   you are supposed to spend at a minimum 2 hours for every hour in class according to SJSU policy.
   that's the minimum.
   find a study group and study together.
   make sure you bring your ideas.
   this is the beginning of your professional network.
   connect on linkedin.
   join clubs --- you'll learn a lot about working with others.
6. **get a laptop for school (not for games)!**
7. **hone your skills!**
8. **AI is not for learning!**
9. **stay healthy!**
10. **don't cheat!**
11. **prepare for senior project early!**
    yeah i know, this is 11, but hey, off-by-one errors are our thing!
    engineering degrees give you a fantastic opportunity to do a senior project that highlights your skills.
    if done correctly, you will have something fantastic to discuss in your job interviews.
    to make that happen you will need a good group, a good topic, and a good advisor.
    find your group at the start of your junior year and get an idea of a topic.
    find your advisor and lock in your topic at the beginning of your 2nd semester of your junior year.
    good advisors will work with you to come up with a topic that you and the advisor are interested in and your advisor has the background to help you on your project.
