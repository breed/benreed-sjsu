About

I was born in Arizona and moved to Ohio as a child, where I developed a lasting dislike of snow. I attended Miami University in Ohio, spent two years in Spain, and earned my master's degree from DePaul University in Chicago while working in IT at Sears and Motorola.

I completed my Ph.D. at UC Santa Cruz and subsequently held research positions at IBM Almaden Research Center. I later transitioned to Yahoo Research before that division was shut down. I then joined Osmeta, a startup I found promising, which was eventually acquired by Facebook. At Facebook, I worked on projects aligned with the company's mission.

In 2018, I fulfilled a long-held aspiration when I joined San José State University's Computer Science faculty. All of my dreams have come true: a faculty position in Silicon Valley.

Research Interests

My research focuses on distributed systems, cloud computing, big data, networking, storage, and operating systems. I actively mentor graduate and undergraduate students on projects in these areas.
